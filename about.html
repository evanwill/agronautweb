The Argonaut is the award winning student newspaper of the University of Idaho, produced by the Department of Student Involvement Student Media. The first issues were released in 1889 and it has been in print ever since. It is typically published twice a week during the academic year. A treasure trove of university happenings, opinions, and local advertizements, these pages give us a glimps into the details of campus life through the century.

At a meeting of the student body in the school year of '98-'99, it was decided to publish a college paper, and a committee was appointed to investigate the cost and to draw up a Constitution and By-Laws. The committee's report was favorably received at the second meeting, and Mr. Guy W. Wolfe was elected Editor-in-Chief and Business Manager. The new paper was christened "The Argonaut, the Searcher of the Golden Fleece." It is to the sagacity and business ability of the first Editor-in-Chief, that we owe the establishment of the Argonaut upon a firm basis. (Gem of the Mountains 1903, p.127)

From Gem of the Mountains 1903

This digital collection provides access to PDFs of each issue from 1898 to 2010. The files were created by digitizing the microfilm copies held by University of Idaho Library's Special Collections and Archives. The PDFs have transcripts produced by Optical Character Recognition, allowing the full text to be searched. Additional metadata was provided by an index created to provide access to the physical collections.

More recent issues of the Argonaut are available on Issuu or the current ARG website.

The Argonaut, is, as it should be, the spokeman of the student body. Every phase of student life is represented by a correspondent. (Gem of the Mountains 1903, p.127)

"Student media serves as a focal point for student life among UI Students. We provide important forums for the exchange of ideas and opinions, information about events and news of general interest about the campus, region, nation and the world."(Gem of the Mountains 2004, p.199)
