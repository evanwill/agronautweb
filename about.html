The Argonaut

The Argonaut is the award winning student newspaper of the University of Idaho, currently produced by the Department of Student Involvement Student Media. It has been continuously in print since 1889, typically published twice a week during the academic year and distributed across campus and the town of Moscow, Idaho.

A treasure trove of university happenings, opinions, and local advertizements, these pages offer us a facinating glimps into the details of campus life through the century. The Argonaut Student Newspaper digital collection complements the Gem of the Mountain Yearbook Collection and Campus Photograph Collection to illustrate the rich history of University of Idaho.

"At a meeting of the student body in the school year of '98-'99, it was decided to publish a college paper, and a committee was appointed to investigate the cost and to draw up a Constitution and By-Laws... ...The new paper was christened "The Argonaut, the Searcher of the Golden Fleece." It is to the sagacity and business ability of the first Editor-in-Chief, that we owe the establishment of the Argonaut upon a firm basis." (Gem of the Mountains 1903, p.127)

The Collection

This digital collection provides access to nearly 6,000 PDFs representing issues of the Argonaut from 1898 to 2010. The files were created by digitizing the microfilm copies held by University of Idaho Library's Special Collections and Archives. The PDFs have transcripts produced by Optical Character Recognition, allowing the full text to be searched. Additional metadata was provided by a partial index orginally created to provide access to the physical collections.

More recent issues of the Argonaut are available on Issuu or the current ARG website.

"Student media serves as a focal point for student life among UI Students. We provide important forums for the exchange of ideas and opinions, information about events and news of general interest about the campus, region, nation and the world."(Gem of the Mountains 2004, p.199)

Technical

This website was created by UI Library's Digital Initiatives using the open source static site generator Jekyll. Layout of the randomized news items on the home page is done using Metafizzy's Packery. Table functionality is added using Jonny Strömberg's List.js. We appreciate and value the open source community that makes these tools available to the world!
